## Why These Tests Matter

This batch targets the three highest-risk areas introduced in the v3.0 release cycle:

**Idempotent grant integrity (GrantLedger).** The ledger was introduced on Jun 1 to prevent duplicate item rewards on refresh, reconnect, or duplicate socket traffic. It is wired into travel, crafting, discoveries, and meals — every reward path in the module. A regression here silently doubles player loot or silently eats grants. The 27 tests validate the core `grantOnce` contract, serialization round-trips (critical for rest-state persistence), and the `hasCraftingForActor` prefix-match guard that prevents double-crafting.

**Comfort homebrew gate (ComfortCalculator).** The `enableComfort` toggle was added May 31 and gates exhaustion DCs, HD penalties, HP caps, and activity DC modifiers. When disabled, all downstream comfort mechanics must short-circuit to safe/neutral values. A single missed gate means players take exhaustion saves they shouldn't, or lose hit dice they should keep. The 32 tests cover every lookup function in both enabled and disabled states, plus the `boostComfort` tier-stepping logic that CampGearScanner and RestSetupApp depend on.

**Well Fed buff regression (MealPhaseHandler).** The Jun 3 hotfix (8d72f64) restored `summaryLine` returns for advantage and resistance buffs in `_buffToActiveEffectPartsAsync`. The existing tests covered `_buffSummaryLabel` but not the parallel AE-building path. The 9 new tests directly assert that all three buff types (advantage, resistance, temp_hp) produce `summaryLine` values, which would have caught the original regression before release.
